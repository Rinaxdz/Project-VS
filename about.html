Our Story

Where the
ninang tradition began

Ninang's On-The-Go Tots was created with one simple goal: to care for children the way family would. The word ninang means godmother in Filipino — someone loving, trusted, and always there when needed. That same spirit guides everything we do.

"In the Philippines, a ninang is more than a godmother — she is a second mother, a trusted guardian, a family pillar. She shows up. She loves without condition. She cares like it's her own."

Ninang's On-The-Go Tots was founded by a Filipino woman who migrated to Canada and carried that tradition with her. In Filipino culture, godparents don't just show up for baptisms — they are woven into the fabric of raising a child. They are called on at celebrations, at milestones, at the moments that matter most.

That's exactly what we bring to your events. Not just childcare — but the warmth, the attentiveness, and the love of a ninang. We believe raising children takes a village, and we're honoured to be part of yours.
